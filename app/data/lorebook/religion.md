# Celestial House
Type: category
Tags: celestials, gods, pantheon, righteousness

The Celestial House is the pantheon of divine beings residing within the Celestial Plane, the highest plane of Coia. These gods embody perfection within their domains, representing righteousness, care, and divine order.

Each member holds absolute authority within their domain and stands in direct opposition to a counterpart within the Void House. Their power is strongest within the Celestial Plane, diminished in the Material Plane, and weakest within the Void.

Mortals who exemplify aspects of a Celestial’s domain may be elevated to sainthood, becoming divine agents capable of performing miracles and influencing events.

---

# Sainthood
Type: system
Tags: celestials, saints, divine, miracles

Sainthood is a divine status granted by members of the Celestial House to mortals who perfectly embody an aspect of their domain.

Saints act as extensions of their patron deity, capable of influencing the Material Plane, performing miracles, and guiding mortals. Each saint represents a focused aspect of their deity’s power rather than the full breadth of the domain.

---

# Zarath
Type: deity
Tags: time, fate, fortune, balance

Zarath is the god of time, fate, and fortune, the keeper of the Tomes of Fate in which all events are recorded. Every action, thought, and outcome exists within his domain.

Unlike other Celestials, Zarath exists across all planes, as time itself is universal. He guides reality toward balance, ensuring that both good and evil exist so that righteousness may ultimately prevail.

His followers are often oracles and visionaries who accept fate as predetermined, occasionally receiving visions to carry out his designs.

---

# Hina
Type: deity
Tags: sea, stars, navigation, life

Hina is the goddess of the sea and stars, a nurturing force that governs both oceans and the space between worlds. She sustains life through her bounty and guides travelers across water and sky.

Her presence is maternal and life-giving, shaping coastal cultures and seafaring traditions. Nearly all maritime peoples revere her in some form.

Her Archeon, the Leviathan, represents the untamed and destructive forces of her domain.

---

# Gale
Type: deity
Tags: air, storm, wind, flight

Gale is the goddess of wind, storms, and flight. She is the breath of all living things and the force behind weather and movement through the skies.

Joyous and free-spirited, she embodies motion and change, bringing both gentle breezes and devastating storms.

Her Archeon, the Thunderbird, represents the raw and destructive force of storms.

---

# Ignia
Type: deity
Tags: earth, stone, forge, stability

Ignia is the goddess of earth, stone, and creation through labor. She is the foundation of the physical world, from soil and mountains to molten rock and metal.

She is steadfast and unyielding, valuing strength, endurance, and self-reliance. Her worship is especially prominent among dwarves and craftsmen.

Her Archeon, the Titan, represents the unstoppable force of earth itself.

---

# Aurora
Type: deity
Tags: righteousness, purity, soul, justice

Aurora is the embodiment of righteousness and purity. She represents moral good, holy power, and the cleansing of corruption.

She is both compassionate and wrathful, guiding mortals toward goodness while empowering them to destroy evil. She is the most widely worshipped Celestial.

Her followers include knights, clergy, and anyone who seeks to oppose darkness.

---

# Sylvana
Type: deity
Tags: nature, life, balance, beasts

Sylvana is the goddess of nature, life, and the natural cycle. She governs all living things and the balance between growth and decay.

She is a caretaker who values harmony within the natural world. Her followers include druids, hunters, and farmers who respect the balance of life.

Her Archeon, the Elderwood, represents the raw and impartial force of nature.

---

# Celestara
Type: deity
Tags: death, afterlife, soul, passage

Celestara is the goddess of death and the guide of souls to the afterlife. She ensures that all souls reach their proper end.

She is calm and inevitable, bringing peace to those who pass. Unlike other Celestials, she has no saints, as her role requires no intermediaries.

Her followers are scholars and those who accept the necessity of death.

---

# Overra
Type: deity
Tags: knowledge, arcane, wisdom, discovery

Overra is the goddess of knowledge, magic, and discovery. She governs arcane forces and the pursuit of understanding.

She encourages curiosity and innovation, guiding scholars, inventors, and spellcasters alike.

Her domain spans all structured knowledge, from magic to science, and she is widely revered by those who seek mastery of their craft.

---

# Void House
Type: category
Tags: void, dark gods, corruption, evil

The Void House is the pantheon of dark gods residing within the Void, the lowest plane of Coia. These beings embody corruption, destruction, and malice within their domains.

Each Void deity opposes a Celestial counterpart. Their power is strongest within the Void, weaker in the Material Plane, and weakest in the Celestial Plane.

They grant power through the Profani, mortals elevated into demigod-like servants who embody their corruption.

---

# The Profani
Type: system
Tags: void, profani, corruption, demigods

The Profani are mortals elevated by the Void House after perfectly embodying aspects of corruption.

They act as agents of their patron deity, spreading influence, granting dark power, and corrupting others. Each represents a specific manifestation of their god’s domain.

---

# Thion
Type: deity
Tags: madness, illusion, deception

Thion is the god of madness, illusion, and deception. He thrives on lies and the unraveling of sanity, leading mortals into ruin through falsehoods.

His followers are unstable and destructive, often consumed by the very madness they spread.

---

# Thyssara
Type: deity
Tags: abyss, despair, sea corruption

Thyssara is the goddess of despair and the corruption of the seas and mind. She creates monstrosities and drives mortals into hopelessness.

Her Malarch, the Deep Hunger, represents the ultimate form of abyssal horror.

---

# Slaros
Type: deity
Tags: rage, destruction, chaos

Slaros is the god of rage and destruction. He embodies uncontrollable fury and the urge to tear down all order.

His followers are violent and short-lived, consumed by their own destructive impulses.

---

# Nareshk
Type: deity
Tags: rot, plague, monstrosities

Nareshk is the goddess of rot, disease, and unnatural creatures. She seeks to destroy life through decay and monstrosities.

Her Malarch, the Typhon, represents the ultimate fusion of monstrous forms.

---

# Iskathis
Type: deity
Tags: corruption, curses, temptation

Iskathis is the goddess of corruption and curses. She tempts mortals into darkness and revels in their fall from grace.

She is responsible for many cursed beings such as vampires and liches.

---

# Vhorrak
Type: deity
Tags: blood, slaughter, violence

Vhorrak is the god of bloodshed and the pleasure of killing. He embodies violence and the thrill of causing pain.

His followers include killers, warbands, and blood mages.

---

# Syrzatha
Type: deity
Tags: lust, obsession, control

Syrzatha is the goddess of obsession, desire, and control. She tempts mortals into surrendering to their darkest urges.

Her influence often begins subtly, leading to eventual corruption and loss of self.

---

# Noctyss
Type: deity
Tags: darkness, doubt, forgotten

Noctyss is the god of darkness, doubt, and oblivion. She represents the loss of knowledge, identity, and purpose.

Her followers embrace ignorance and seek the dissolution of self within darkness.
