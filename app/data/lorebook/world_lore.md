# Terra
Type: place
Tags: world, terra, material-plane

Terra is the primary world within the Material Plane of Coia, home to the vast majority of mortal civilizations. It is a large and diverse planet, containing multiple continents, climates, and cultures shaped by history, religion, and the lingering effects of ancient conflicts.

The peoples of Terra live under the influence of the Celestial and Void Houses, though direct divine intervention is rare and often subtle. Magic exists but is structured, studied, and controlled in most developed regions, particularly within major nations and institutions. Civilization is advanced to a late industrial equivalent, with rail systems, organized states, and expanding global interaction.

Terra serves as the central stage upon which the struggles between order, corruption, knowledge, and ambition play out, shaped by both mortal action and the distant influence of the planes beyond.

# Coia
Type: place
Tags: realm, coia, planes

Coia is the greater realm that contains all planes of existence, including Terra. It is composed of layered planes held together within the Outer Plane, which acts as a stabilizing boundary against the chaotic, infinite arcana beyond.

Within Coia exist the major planes: the Celestial Plane, Fey Plane, Material Plane, Underbrim, and Void. Each plays a role in the balance of existence, with the Celestial and Void Houses acting as opposing forces that influence reality through mortals and lesser beings.

All life, magic, and structure exist within Coia, making it the totality of known existence to mortals and scholars alike.

# Ardona
Type: place
Tags: continent, ardona, new-world

Ardona is a vast continent across the western seas, considered a frontier compared to the older civilizations of the Twin Continents. It is divided into northern and southern regions, both of which are still undergoing exploration, colonization, and conflict.

The land is home to powerful native civilizations as well as expanding settlements from overseas powers. Trade, resource extraction, and cultural clashes define much of Ardona’s modern history. Its scale and relative lack of centralized control make it a place of opportunity, danger, and rapid change.

# The Twin Continents
Type: place
Tags: continent, twin-continents, core

The Twin Continents form the central heart of global civilization on Terra. Divided into a western and eastern landmass, they are home to the most established nations, institutions, and political powers.

These continents have long histories of war, alliance, and cultural development. They are the origin of many of the world’s dominant religions, arcane institutions, and governing systems. Trade routes, diplomacy, and rivalry between nations here shape much of the global balance of power.

# Sundjara
Type: place
Tags: continent, sundjara

Sundjara is a massive southern continent defined by diverse climates and long-standing civilizations. Northern regions are dominated by powerful desert empires and trade networks, while central areas contain rich and varied kingdoms.

The southeastern regions are considered the ancient homeland of several elder races, though they are now largely isolationist. Sundjara is known for its deep history, strong cultural identity, and relatively slower technological progression compared to the Twin Continents.

# Tanshir
Type: place
Tags: continent, tanshir

Tanshir is a vast eastern continent known for its strong magical traditions and distinct cultural development. While some regions lag behind in industrial advancement, they often surpass others in arcane knowledge and practice.

The continent is composed of multiple kingdoms and regions, each with its own traditions and governing systems. Magic is more openly integrated into daily life in many parts of Tanshir, leading to unique approaches to education, warfare, and society.

# The Eastern Isles
Type: place
Tags: islands, eastern-isles, ocean

The Eastern Isles are a scattered chain of islands extending beyond Tanshir into the open ocean. These islands are home to seafaring cultures deeply connected to the ocean and sky, often relying on navigation, oral tradition, and strong communal identity.

The peoples of the Isles maintain a close relationship with natural forces, particularly the sea and stars. Their cultures emphasize navigation, survival, and respect for the balance of the world, and they are often seen as some of the most skilled sailors on Terra.

# Magic in Coia
Type: concept
Tags: magic, arcana, soul, spellcasting

Magic in Coia is the act of using one’s soul to channel arcana into a controlled effect. Arcana exists throughout the planes as a fundamental force, but it cannot be shaped without a conduit. The soul serves as that conduit, allowing mortals to draw, direct, and refine arcane energy into spells and other effects.

The strength, control, and complexity of magic are determined by the individual’s soul. A stronger or more refined soul allows for greater output and precision, while an untrained or unstable soul can lead to failure or dangerous backlash. Because of this, formal training is required in most advanced societies, and magic is treated as a discipline rather than an instinct.

Spellcasting is therefore not simply the act of knowing a spell, but the act of shaping arcana through will, understanding, and internal capacity. Every casting places strain on the soul, and overextension can lead to exhaustion or long-term damage if not properly managed.

# Enchanting in Coia
Type: concept
Tags: enchanting, soul, runes, arcana

Enchanting is the process of binding a soul to an object using runic structures in order to grant that object a sustained magical function. Unlike spellcasting, which relies on the caster’s active use of their own soul, enchanting creates a permanent or semi-permanent effect by anchoring arcane function within a physical form.

This process requires a soul as a power source. The soul is bound into the object through carefully inscribed runes, which define the behavior, limits, and function of the enchantment. Without a soul, true enchantment cannot occur—objects may be temporarily imbued with magic, but they will not sustain it.

The quality of an enchantment depends on both the strength of the soul used and the precision of the runic design. Poorly constructed runes can lead to unstable or dangerous effects, while flawed binding can result in the degradation or collapse of the enchantment over time.

Because enchanting requires the use of souls, it is a heavily regulated and ethically debated practice in many parts of Coia. Some see it as a necessary advancement of magical technology, while others view it as a violation of the natural order.

# The Eos Republic
Type: faction
Tags: faction, government, republic, eos

The Eos Republic is the governing body of the island, structured around a senate composed primarily of human representatives. It oversees law, trade, and military organization across the island.

While the republic presents itself as unified, each city holds its own economic importance, creating an internal balance of power between regions.

# Elven Origins of Eos
Type: lore
Tags: lore, elves, history, eos

Before the arrival of humans, Eos was inhabited primarily by elves. They built stone structures deep within the forests, many of which still stand today as ruins.

These ruins are characterized by geometric layouts, open-air designs, and alignment with natural features. Their original purpose is not fully understood, though they are believed to have held cultural, spiritual, or arcane significance.

---

# Human Settlement of Eos
Type: lore
Tags: lore, history, humans, eos

Humans arrived on Eos generations ago, establishing coastal settlements that eventually grew into major cities. Over time, they formed a unified republic, consolidating control over trade and governance.

While elves still inhabit the island, they are no longer the dominant political force, leading to subtle cultural and historical tensions.
